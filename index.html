How dicotic feels

Three steps to divided listening.

dicotic gives you a gentle, controllable way to explore how your brain responds to multiple streams at once—no hacks, no noise, just experiments.

1

Pick your two streams.

Choose your vibe and your brain food. A playlist that keeps you moving, plus a podcast, audiobook, or news source you want to follow.

2

Assign each ear.

Send music to the ear that keeps you in motion, and words to the ear that keeps you learning. Discover which ear prefers rhythm and which prefers language.

3

Tap to reset your focus.

When things feel noisy, a single tap swaps sides or centers your focus. Find the balance between energy and comprehension in real time.

Behind the idea

Inspired by how attention really feels.

Some people focus best when part of their mind is anchored by rhythm while another part tracks words. dicotic doesn't claim magic shortcuts or brain hacks—it simply gives you an intentional way to explore how your own attention behaves.

By separating streams into left and right ears, you can learn when your brain wants simplicity, when it enjoys a gentle challenge, and when it's time to reset. Over time, those small experiments become a kind of map of how you work best.
